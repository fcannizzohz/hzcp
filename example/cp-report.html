Event types: what they mean and why they matter
Quick glossary of extracted event types. Use this to interpret charts and “from my seat” tables.
How to use
  • Start with cp_snapshot and role_observed to understand intended CP membership.
  • Use vote_*, pre_vote_*, term_moved, and election_timeout to explain leader churn.
  • Correlate tcp_* with timeouts and rejections to separate network issues from overload.
event_type Meaning Why it matters Common interpretation / next checks
cp_snapshot Observed CP group membership snapshot. Ground truth of who is in the group at that moment. Divergence across seats implies partial visibility or log gaps.
role_observed Member role seen inside a snapshot (LEADER/FOLLOWER). Used to reconstruct leader history. Rapid role changes indicate churn.
leader_set Leader UUID set or observed. Signals leadership transitions. High frequency = instability.
we_are_leader Node claims leadership. Strong leader transition signal. Multiple seats claiming ≈ split or jitter.
vote_granted Vote granted to candidate. Normal during elections, bad if constant. Correlate with network and lag signals.
vote_rejected Vote explicitly rejected. Clear election failure evidence. Check rejection reasons and connectivity.
pre_vote_rejected Pre-vote rejected with reason. Most actionable election diagnostic. Often points to lagging logs or unreachable majority.
term_moved Raft term advanced. Tracks election churn. Frequent moves without progress = instability.
election_timeout Election did not complete in time. Direct liveness failure signal. Check GC, CPU, and network.
tcp_connect_timeout TCP connection attempt timed out. Strong network-path indicator. If paired with vote failures, blame network first.
invocation_timeout Operation invocation timed out. System not making progress. Correlate with GC pauses and executor saturation.
